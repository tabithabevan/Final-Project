Alumni

Learn from athletes who've made successful transitions

We work directly with universities to build alumni networks!

The Alumni Mentorship Program is designed to provide retiring collegiate athletes with the support they need as they transition away from their sports careers. Recognizing that the shift from competitive athletics to life after sports can be overwhelming, this program connects current athletes with alumni who have successfully navigated that transition. Athletes can request a one-on-one alumni mentor, fostering a personalized relationship where they can share experiences, seek advice, and receive guidance tailored to their individual needs. These mentors offer valuable insights, sharing their own journeys and coping strategies for managing the challenges that come with retiring from sport.

In addition to individual mentorship, the program also offers opportunities for athletes to engage in larger group settings. Alumni speak at organized events, sharing their stories and lessons learned with a wider audience of athletes. These talks are designed to create a sense of community, showing athletes that they are not alone in their experiences. By hearing from those who have been through similar challenges, athletes can gain perspective, find inspiration, and better understand the emotional and psychological aspects of retiring from sports. These group interactions encourage open dialogue, allowing athletes to ask questions, discuss their concerns, and form connections that extend beyond the formal program.

Ultimately, the goal of the Alumni Mentorship Program is to ease the emotional and psychological burden of retirement by providing athletes with the tools and support they need to thrive in life after sports. Through the shared experience of alumni, athletes can build a support network that not only helps them transition but also empowers them to find new purpose and fulfillment.
